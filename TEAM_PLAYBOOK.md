# Team playbook — running a legal AI enablement function

How a small team of legal engineers / product specialists takes sophisticated lawyers from curiosity to weekly use, consistently and at quality. Neutral and synthetic; not legal advice.

This playbook exists because adoption is a team sport with a quality bar. It documents how the work is run, reviewed, and escalated — so a new joiner is productive fast and customers get a consistent experience.

## 1. Onboarding a new legal engineer (first 30 days)

| Week | Focus | Done when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Product fluency + the evaluation mindset | Can run the eval harness and explain why a draft is rejected |
| 2 | Shadow two workshops + two customer calls | Can run the workflow-discovery exercise solo |
| 3 | Own a low-risk account with supervision | Delivers a workshop; writes the adoption memo |
| 4 | Own the feedback loop for one account | Files one well-formed product-feedback item |

The bar for "ready": can stand in front of partners, run discovery, and leave the room with a prioritised pilot — not a demo.

## 2. Weekly customer-success rhythm

- **Monday:** review adoption signals; flag at-risk accounts (low usage, stalled practice group, renewal window).
- **Midweek:** one proactive touch per at-risk account; capture blockers.
- **Friday:** update each account's adoption memo; escalate product feedback; refresh the pipeline view.

The weekly artifact is the account adoption memo — health, usage by practice group, blockers, next action.

## 3. How to review demo / workshop quality

Before a session is "good enough" to run with a customer, it clears:
1. **Relevance** — tasks use the customer's real-shaped workflows, not generic ones.
2. **Honesty** — review-risk is named, not hidden; the pilot chosen is safe to automate first.
3. **Outcome** — the session ends with a prioritised pilot and a measurable success metric.
4. **Follow-through** — a 30-day plan with an owner and a check-in cadence.

## 4. How to coach workshop delivery

- Watch for the failure mode of "demo theatre" — impressive output, no workflow change. Redirect to discovery.
- Coach the discovery questions, not the slides: frequency, pain, review-risk, who owns the workflow.
- Debrief every session against the four quality gates above; pick one thing to improve next time.

## 5. How to escalate product feedback

1. Capture the user pain in their words.
2. Classify by **severity** and **revenue/renewal impact**.
3. Map pain → product requirement (so engineering gets a requirement, not an anecdote).
4. Route to product with a one-paragraph memo; track what shipped and close the loop with the customer ("what we heard, what we changed").

## 6. How to document adoption blockers

Keep a single, shared blockers list per account: blocker, root cause (product / training / trust / workflow), owner, status. Patterns across accounts become the prioritised product-feedback list.

## 7. Consistent customer messaging

- One source of truth for value framing and risk posture (no freelancing on legal claims).
- Never overclaim: no "compliance guaranteed," no "replaces lawyers." The message is *faster, with judgment kept in the loop*.
- Escalate anything that sounds like legal advice to a qualified lawyer.

## 8. Quality standards for legal AI enablement

Every customer-facing artifact is: relevant to their workflow, honest about review-risk, measurable in outcome, and safe in its claims. If it fails any of these, it does not ship.
